# Book Method Distiller

Use this skill when turning book notes, reading highlights, or long-form methodology notes into durable reusable methods.

## Rules

- Treat the note as source material, not as proof that the method is universally true.
- Preserve evidence references from the input blocks, such as `B1`, `B2`, and `B3`.
- Separate stable principles, decision rules, mental models, operational checks, boundaries, anti-patterns, source gaps, and open questions.
- Prefer long-lived mechanisms over timely opinions.
- Keep the author's idea separate from Seed's own interpretation.
- Do not convert a general principle into trading, investing, medical, legal, or other high-stakes advice unless the source explicitly supports that use case.
- If a principle is interesting but weakly supported by the note, mark confidence as `low` and add the missing evidence to source gaps.

## Output Priorities

- What method can be reused.
- When the method applies.
- What inputs or observations the method needs.
- What breaks the method.
- What an agent should check before using it.
- How this method can be compared with creator profiles, video semantics, news facts, earnings facts, or finance digests.
